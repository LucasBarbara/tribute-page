Marie Curie

Woman and pioneering scientist

Marie Curie was a French-Polish physicist and chemist known by her pioneering research on radioactivity. She discovered the elements radium and polonium and helped fight against cancer. Her lifetime work granted her numerous awards including a nobel prize, the first of his kind to be awarded to a woman.

Marie was born in Warsaw, 5th child of an impoverished family. Growing up, she wanted to pursuit higher education studies which were, at the time, not available for women in Poland. Despite this unfavorable beginning, she grew to become one of the most important scientists of her time

Marie was the first person and only woman to win a nobel twice and is the only person that has won a nobel in two different sciences. During her career, she published numerous papers alone and together with her husband and was the first woman to be a professor at the University of Paris. Her many achievements include the development of the theory of radiation, the founding of the Radium Institute of the University of Paris and the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw.

Here's some relevant dates of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw on 7 November
  • 1890 - Began practical scientific training in a chemical laboratory at the Museum of Industry and Agriculture, in Warsaw
  • 1891 - Moved to France where she enrolled in the University of Paris to continue her studies of physics, chemistry and mathematics
  • 1893 - Was awarded a degree in physics
  • 1894 - Earned a second degree, in mathematics, by the University of Paris
  • 1894 - Met Pierre Curie, an instructor at the School of Physics and Chemistry
  • 1895 - Married Pierre Curie, who received his own doctorate earlier that same year
  • 1897 - Her daughter, Irène, was born. Began teaching at the École Normale Supérieure
  • 1898 - Discovered that the element thorium was radioactive.
  • 1898 - Discovered, as a couple, the existence of polonium and radium
  • 1900 - Become the first woman faculty member at the École Normale Supérieure
  • 1903 - Was awarded her doctorate from the University of Paris
  • 1903 - Won the Nobel Prize in Physics, together with Pierre Currie and Henri Becquerel
  • 1904 - Gives birth to her second daughter
  • 1906 - Lost her husband, Pierre Curie, in a road accident
  • 1906 - Became a Professor of General Physics at the University of Paris, stepping into her late husband chair
  • 1910 - Succefully isolated radium
  • 1911 - Won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry, becoming the first person to win two nobel prizes
  • 1914 - Set up France's first military radiology center
  • 1922 - Became a fellow of the French Academy of Medicine
  • 1923 - Wrote Pierre Curie biography, entitled Pierre Curie
  • 1930 - Was elected a member of the International Atomic Weights Committee
  • 1934 - Died, aged 66, of aplastic anemia caused by exposure to radiation
